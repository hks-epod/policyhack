PolicyHack

The open data hackathon

18-19 April, 2015

What is "POLICYHACKS" ?

Do you want to develop software applications that improve the way the Indian government works? Do you want to write code that helps government work more efficiently, decreases corruption, and ensures that all Indians can benefit from the big data revolution? We know you’re out there: you are one of India’s elite coders, and you have the right skills to put data to work for your country.

If this sounds like you, come join us at our first ever "PolicyHack", where we’ll be bringing India’s top coders together with policy makers and government partners to work on real-world problems that stand in the way of a more effective, efficient, and transparent government. Come join us to write applications, create practical visualizations, and solve some of the Indian government’s most difficult data-related challenges. PolicyHack will give you the opportunity to interact directly with mentors from the top tiers of the policy world to understand what types of solutions can meet their challenges, giving you the unique opportunity to build applications that are immediately relevant and applicable to the challenges that stand in the way of better government

What should I expect?

Interested applicants should submit an application to join us as coders in the PolicyHack. If selected, you will be invited to participate in our two-day hackathon on Saturday, April 18 and Sunday, April 19. Policy problem tracks will be posted within three days of the PolicyHack, giving you time to consider your options and brainstorm ideas before showing up to the event and choosing your track. Upon arrival, you will have the option to form a team with other developers if you wish. From 10 am Saturday through 3:30 pm Sunday, you and your team will build your own prototype according to the track of your choice. You will then have the opportunity to present your solution to a panel of judges combining technical and policy experience.

PRIZES

Winners of each policy problem track will receive a prize of Rs. 20 000. The winner(s) of the grand prize will receive space in Evidence for Policy Design’s Delhi office for a two month incubation and mentoring period with our technical team. Following the two month incubation period, we will send you to our home office at Harvard University in Cambridge, Massachusetts (USA), to spend a week with our top policy and technical team to gain exposure to more cutting-edge work at the intersection of policy and technology.
